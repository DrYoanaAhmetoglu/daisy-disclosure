About DAISY

Many journals and conferences now require authors to disclose whether and how AI tools were used in preparing a manuscript, but guidance varies widely. DAISY supports authors in generating a clear, structured AI disclosure statement that can be adapted to different venues.

An AI disclosure is a short statement describing whether AI tools contributed to the research or writing process and, if so, how. It supports transparency for readers, editors, and reviewers and is typically included in the Acknowledgements or a dedicated AI use / Declarations section, depending on venue policy.

Here, AI tools refer to systems that generate, transform, or analyse content using machine-learning models, such as language models or AI-assisted coding and analysis tools. Standard software (e.g., word processors or reference managers) is not considered AI unless it includes generative or model-based features, such as automatic text generation, rewriting, summarisation, or predictive content suggestions.

Based on your selections, DAISY generates a draft disclosure statement reflecting common practices. Authors should review and edit it as needed to ensure accuracy and alignment with their target journal or conference’s AI policy. Authors remain fully responsible for the work.

DAISY is part of a research project led by Yoana Ahmetoglu (UCL), Marios Constantinidis (CYENS Centre of Excellence), and Anna Cox (UCL). Feedback or questions can be sent to yoana.ahmetoglu@ucl.ac.uk.
